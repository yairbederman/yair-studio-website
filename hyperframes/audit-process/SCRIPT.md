# SCRIPT — what the film says (v3)

Subject: the **AI Workflow Audit** — it takes a messy workflow and separates the work
into three kinds, then defines a first system. A process film, not a sales ad. Reductive
and type-led: the words are few and large.

## The message arc

> A workflow is messy by default. The audit reads it — and the real call isn't "automate
> everything." Some things automate. Some things assist. Some things stay human. That
> separation *is* your first useful system.

## The lines (the entire script — large Inter, one idea per beat)

| Beat | Line |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Messy by default.** |
| 2 | **The audit reads it.** |
| 3 (peak) | **Some things automate.** / **Some things assist.** / **Some things _stay human._** |
| 4 | **Your first useful system.** |

Plus one persistent corner wordmark: `AI WORKFLOW AUDIT`. Nothing else — no captions,
no chips, no indices. Every word is large and high-contrast.

## The peak — three kinds of work (the "aha")

The heart of the film. The ordered field of work splits into three rows as three lines
reveal in turn — **automate**, **assist**, **stay human**. "Stay human" lands last, in
copper, as its tiles warm to copper under a steady held glow. The brand's promise —
*some things stay human* — is the resolved note, placed at the decision point. The beat
**holds ~1.8s** so all three kinds read.

## The payoff — first useful system

The three kinds consolidate into one tidy block (the human tiles stay copper, with a
small steady copper ✓ — human approval, wordless). Headline: **Your first useful system.**
The viewer leaves with: the audit doesn't automate everything — it gives you a scoped
system that keeps people where judgment matters.

## Honesty rules (non-negotiable)
- No numbers, names, logos, or fabricated UI. Tiles are abstract work units, not data.
- Every line traces to the audit's actual method (read the workflow → separate the work →
  scope a first system). Nothing invented.
