# SCRIPT — what the film says

Subject: the four offers as **equal entry points into one operating system**.
Offer-neutral by design.

## The message arc

> Different entry points → the same operating layer. Clear next actions, with
> human approval where it matters.

## The four lanes (signals trace to `offers.ts`)

| Lane | Offer | Signals shown |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **AI Workflow Audit** | map workflow · find friction · choose first useful system |
| 2 | **Internal AI Systems** | assistant layer · office memory · approval-aware drafts |
| 3 | **Dashboards & Automation** | open loops · stuck items · weekly signal |
| 4 | **Content & Ad Operations** | content pipeline · campaign rhythm · review queue |

The four are presented as **peers** — same weight, same treatment — and converge
into one core. No sequence, no hierarchy, no elevated wedge.

## The peak — human approval at the junction

Where the four lanes meet, a copper checkpoint **holds**: the system pauses,
approval confirms (✓ Approved), and only then does the unified system start
running. This is the brand's promise — automation never runs end to end on its
own — placed at the literal convergence point.

## The payoff — one operating system

After approval: **One operating system** with **clear next actions** (abstract
task rows) and a **weekly operating rhythm** (a 7-segment bar that fills once).
The settled frame communicates: *one system, clear next actions, human approval
where it matters.*

## Honesty rules (non-negotiable)

- No numbers, percentages, names, or logos. Rows and the weekly bar are
  **structural placeholders**, not data.
- Every lane label + signal traces to the offer summaries in `offers.ts`. Nothing
  invented.
