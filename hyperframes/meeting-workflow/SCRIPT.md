# SCRIPT — what the film says

Subject: the **Meeting Follow-up** workflow already mapped on `/workflows`. Offer-neutral
(takes no position on the undecided primary wedge); it demonstrates the *method*.

## The message arc

> Scattered inputs become a clear sequence of next actions — and a person approves
> before anything goes out.

## Beats

| # | Step (node) | What the viewer reads | Evidence produced |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Meeting notes** | The raw inputs of a meeting, scattered across apps | `transcript · recording · notes` converge in |
| 2 | **Extract decisions + tasks** | The system reads structure out of the noise | `decision` / `task` chips |
| 3 | **Assign owner + date** | Every item gets an owner and a date | `owner · date` chip |
| 4 | **Draft follow-up** | A follow-up is prepared — but only prepared | a draft card (placeholder lines) |
| 5 | **Human approval** ← PEAK | A person decides before anything is sent | **✓ Approved** badge, copper |
| 6 | **Update tasks / dashboard** | Approved work lands as ordered, tracked tasks | task rows resolve from chaos → order |

## Why approval is the peak

It is the brand's core promise — "automation never runs end to end on its own"
(PRODUCT.md principle 5). The film slows, dims everything else, and lets the copper
checkpoint carry the frame for ~2.5s. Nothing in the film is "sent"; the last beat is
*tasks updated*, not *email sent* — the human still owns the send.

## Honesty rules (non-negotiable)

- No numbers, percentages, dates, names, or company logos. The draft card and task rows
  are **structural placeholders**, signalling shape, never fabricated content.
- Every element traces to the real workflow already written on `/workflows`
  (the `MAPPED` node list) — nothing invented.
